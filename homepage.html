Creating Prosperous

Rural Communities

Fighting A Generational Problem

For decades now, drought has ravaged rural Maharashtra. Year on year, rural populations are caught in a cycle of battling thirst, hunger, debt and forced migration. The challenges posed by climate change have worsened this crisis, creating increased uncertainty and economic destabilisation for villages and farmer families.

“We believe that the power to solve the biggest environmental issues lies with the people themselves. Collective action and shramadaan have created miracles in Maharashtra!”

– Aamir Khan, Founder – Paani Foundation

At Paani Foundation, we have experienced first-hand how communities at the grassroots can fight drought and climate change, provided they receive the right knowledge, training and platform to do this work.

Since 2016, our work has been geared towards mobilising, training and incentivising communities in rural Maharashtra to achieve economic and ecological prosperity.

The Paani Way

✔️ Mobilising and training drought-hit villages to work on water conservation

✔️ Creating water-abundance in villages via community-led efforts

✔️ Collectivising farmers to work on sustainable farming and water management

✔️ Training farmers to improve incomes via carbon-and water-efficient practices

✔️ Creating self-reliant and prosperous rural communities
